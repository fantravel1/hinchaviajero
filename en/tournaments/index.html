The biggest club tournament in South America. Teams from 10 countries compete from February to November in a competition that defines legends. Fans from across the continent cross borders to support their team on magical nights.

National Leagues

Every league has its own format, ticketing system, and rules for visiting fans. Here we break down the most important ones.

Argentina

Liga Profesional Argentina

A year-round league with 28 teams. The socio (membership) system is key for getting tickets, and many matches do not allow visiting fans at all. Even so, Argentine derbies are an experience that nothing else compares to.

28 teams Year-round Socio system, no visiting fans in many matches
Must-see derbies: Boca-River, Racing-Independiente, San Lorenzo-Huracan
Mexico

Liga MX

Apertura/Clausura format with 18 teams. Ticketmaster Mexico is the main platform for tickets. Great stadium infrastructure, good connectivity between cities, and a well-organized matchday experience.

18 teams Apertura / Clausura Ticketmaster Mexico, great infrastructure
Must-see derbies: America-Chivas, Cruz Azul-Pumas, Tigres-Monterrey
Brazil

Brasileirao (Serie A)

20 teams spread across a massive geography. The Ingresso Rapido system is the most widely used ticketing platform. Distances between cities are long (prepare for 10+ hour bus rides), but the reward is the most vibrant football on the continent.

20 teams Apr - Dec Ingresso Rapido, long bus distances
Must-see derbies: Flamengo-Fluminense, Corinthians-Palmeiras
Peru

Liga 1 Peru

An accessible and affordable league. Most matches are played in Lima, which simplifies logistics. The league is growing and stadiums are being modernized, but prices remain fan-friendly for the traveling supporter.

19 teams Feb - Nov Affordable, Lima-centric, growing fast
Must-see derby: Universitario-Alianza Lima

Practical Information

What you need to know before planning your trip for any tournament.

Calendar: When each league plays

South American leagues generally run from February to November/December. Liga MX is split into Apertura (Jul-Dec) and Clausura (Jan-May). CONMEBOL continental tournaments (Libertadores and Sudamericana) run from February to November, with the group stage kicking off in April. European league schedules don't apply here: plan your trip between March and November for the best action.

International tickets

For Copa Libertadores and Sudamericana matches, ticketing is managed by CONMEBOL in coordination with each local club. Visiting fan tickets are allocated in limited quantities and are generally sold through the visiting club. Never buy from unauthorized resellers. Check our ticketing guide to avoid scams.

Plan ahead

Continental tournament matches sell out flights and hotels quickly, especially during knockout stages. Book transport and accommodation as soon as the brackets are confirmed. In semifinals and finals, prices can spike by up to 300%. Early planning is not optional: it's the difference between being there and watching on TV.

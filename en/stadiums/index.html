The only stadium to have hosted two World Cup finals. 87,000 souls roaring during the Clasico Nacional. Easy to reach by metro, and the food scene around the stadium is a bonus before kickoff.

Before You Go to the Stadium

Practical tips that apply to any stadium on the continent.

Arrive early

Security checks at Latin American stadiums are stricter for visiting fans. Plan to arrive at least 90 minutes before kickoff to avoid long queues and enjoy the pre-match atmosphere inside the ground.

Cash is king

Most food vendors and street sellers around stadiums only accept cash in local currency. Bring small bills and coins. Cards only work at the official box offices of some modern stadiums.

Away jersey with caution

In high-rivalry matches, many stadiums don't allow entry wearing the visiting team's jersey outside the designated away section. Check before you go. On the streets, avoid wearing your team's colors in neighborhoods associated with the rival.
